### [Grammar/Subject-Verb Agreement] Subject–verb agreement
Singular subjects (he/she/it or a singular noun) take a verb with -s; Chinese learners often drop it.
Wrong: He have great grade. / My mom talk with him.
Correct: He has great grades. / My mom talked with him.
Cue: he/she/it + base verb; singular noun + plural verb.

### [Grammar/Verb Tense] Tense consistency
Maintain a consistent tense; do not switch between past and present without reason.
Wrong: Yesterday I go to school and saw my friend.
Correct: Yesterday I went to school and saw my friend.
Cue: time markers (yesterday, now) mismatched with verb form.

### [Grammar/Articles] Article usage (a / an / the)
Use a/an for non-specific singular count nouns, the for specific ones; do not omit articles.
Wrong: I want to be teacher. / She is best student in class.
Correct: I want to be a teacher. / She is the best student in the class.
Cue: missing article before singular count noun.

### [Grammar/Countable] Countable vs uncountable nouns
Uncountable nouns (information, advice, knowledge, equipment) take no plural -s and no a/an.
Wrong: He gave me many advices and informations.
Correct: He gave me much advice and a lot of information.
Cue: plural -s on uncountable nouns.

### [Grammar/Run-on] Run-on / comma splice
Two independent clauses cannot be joined by a comma alone; use a period, semicolon, or conjunction.
Wrong: Technology is useful, it also has problems.
Correct: Technology is useful, but it also has problems.
Cue: long sentences chaining clauses with commas.

### [Grammar/Word Order] Chinese-influenced word order
English places adjectives before nouns and keeps subject-verb-object order; avoid calquing Chinese order.
Wrong: I very like this book. / He is a my friend.
Correct: I like this book very much. / He is a friend of mine.
Cue: "very + verb"; double determiners.

### [Grammar/Literal Translation] Literal/word-for-word collocations
Some Chinese phrases translate wrongly into English collocations.
Wrong: open the light / eat medicine / people mountain people sea.
Correct: turn on the light / take medicine / a huge crowd.
Cue: unusual verb-noun pairings.

### [Grammar/Prepositions] Preposition errors
Prepositions often differ from Chinese; learners over/under-use them.
Wrong: discuss about the problem / arrive to school / according to my opinion.
Correct: discuss the problem / arrive at school / in my opinion.
Cue: "discuss about", "arrive to", redundant prepositions.

### [Grammar/Plural] Missing plural marking
Count nouns need -s when plural; learners often omit it.
Wrong: There are many student in the school.
Correct: There are many students in the school.
Cue: quantifier (many/several) + singular noun.

### [Grammar/Sentence Fragment] Sentence fragments
Every sentence needs a subject and a finite verb; avoid fragments.
Wrong: Because technology is everywhere. So many problems.
Correct: Because technology is everywhere, many problems arise.
Cue: subordinate clause standing alone.
