### [Rubric/Holistic-1] Holistic 1.0 - Minimal
Extremely limited vocabulary; pervasive basic errors that severely distort meaning; very short, fragmented or repetitive sentences; no clear organization. The text is extremely difficult to understand.

### [Rubric/Holistic-2] Holistic 2.0 - Emerging
Basic high-frequency vocabulary; frequent errors in simple structures (subject-verb agreement, tense); mostly simple sentences; limited logical order with misused linking words. Conveys only a basic idea with effort from the reader.

### [Rubric/Holistic-3] Holistic 3.0 - Developing
Adequate everyday vocabulary; errors are frequent but generally do not obscure meaning; a mix of simple and compound sentences; basic structure with some cohesive devices. Generally understandable despite noticeable errors.

### [Rubric/Holistic-4] Holistic 4.0 - Proficient
Good range of vocabulary including some idiomatic expressions; good grammatical control with few errors; varied sentence structures including complex sentences; clear logical progression. Reads smoothly with only occasional errors.

### [Rubric/Holistic-5] Holistic 5.0 - Advanced
Rich, precise, natural vocabulary; high grammatical accuracy; wide range of sophisticated structures; excellent organization and cohesion. Fully successful in conveying its message.

### [Rubric/Grammar] Grammar dimension
Assesses morphological and grammatical accuracy: verb tense, subject-verb agreement, articles, plurals, prepositions. High scores = errors are rare and do not impede communication.

### [Rubric/Syntax] Syntax dimension
Assesses sentence structure and variety. High scores = varied, well-formed sentences including complex structures; low scores = fragments, run-ons, repetitive simple sentences.

### [Rubric/Vocabulary] Vocabulary dimension
Assesses range, precision, and appropriateness of word choice. High scores = precise and varied lexis; low scores = repetitive, vague, or misused words.

### [Rubric/Cohesion] Cohesion dimension
Assesses organization, logical flow, and use of cohesive devices (however, therefore, first, also). High scores = ideas connect smoothly; low scores = disconnected ideas, missing or misused transitions.

### [Rubric/Conventions] Conventions dimension
Assesses spelling, punctuation, capitalization, and mechanics. High scores = clean mechanics; low scores = frequent spelling/punctuation errors that distract the reader.
